Banff National Park

Is home to a diverse array of wildlife. The park's rugged terrain provides habitat for large mammals such as grizzly bears, black bears, elk, and mountain goats.

Visitors to the park may also spot smaller animals like pikas, marmots, and chipmunks. Banff is also an important bird-watching destination, with over 260 species of birds recorded in the park.

The park's protected status helps to preserve these precious wildlife populations, and visitors are encouraged to enjoy the park's natural wonders while minimizing their impact on the environment.
